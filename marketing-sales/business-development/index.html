Partner Qualification Framework

PARTNER Score:

Criteria Question Weight
Potential What's the upside? 20%
Alignment Do goals/values match? 20%
Reach What audience can they access? 15%
Timing Are they ready now? 15%
Need Do they need what we offer? 15%
Experience Have they done this before? 10%
Risk What could go wrong? 5%

Score 70+: Prioritize actively Score 50-70: Keep warm Score <50: Deprioritize

Partnership Outreach

Outreach Sequence

Phase 1: Research (Before Contact)

  • Company website deep dive
  • Key personnel LinkedIn research
  • Recent news/press releases
  • Existing partnerships they have
  • Mutual connections check
  • Their tech stack (if relevant)

Phase 2: Initial Outreach

Email Template:

Subject: Partnership idea: [Specific value proposition]

Hi [Name],

[Personalized observation showing you did research ‚Äî reference something specific they did/said].

[One sentence on mutual benefit ‚Äî what's in it for them + what's in it for you].

Best,

Proposal Best Practices

  1. Lead with their value ‚Äî Partner's benefits first
  2. Be specific ‚Äî Vague proposals get ignored
  3. Show you understand them ‚Äî Reference their business
  4. Make it easy ‚Äî Clear next steps
  5. Keep it scannable ‚Äî Execs skim
  6. Include social proof ‚Äî Other partnerships, traction
  7. Address risk ‚Äî Show you've thought it through

Best Practices

  1. Research before outreach ‚Äî Personalized > Generic
  2. Lead with value ‚Äî What's in it for them?
  3. Build relationships, not transactions ‚Äî Long-term thinking
  4. Document everything ‚Äî Conversations, decisions, context
  5. Follow up persistently ‚Äî Most deals need 5+ touchpoints
  6. Know your competition ‚Äî Updated battlecards win deals
  7. Start small ‚Äî Pilot before big commitment
  8. Measure what matters ‚Äî Partners closed, not meetings held

Common Mistakes

‚ùå Pitching too early ‚Äî Understand their needs first ‚ùå Overselling ‚Äî Under-promise, over-deliver ‚ùå Ignoring internal champions ‚Äî Find and nurture them ‚ùå No mutual accountability ‚Äî Both sides need commitments ‚ùå Slow follow-up ‚Äî Momentum matters ‚ùå Vague proposals ‚Äî Specific asks get specific answers
